Meet me!

Ambitous. Adaptable. Creative.

If you're on this webpage then you probably know by now that I'm Libby! I have a bachelor's degree in professional and technical writing and a minor in communication. I have successfully learned how to create documents that can help serve their audience and give the reader exactly what they're looking for. During my undergrad career, I did tons of projects that progressed my writing and editing skills. In my free time I enjoy picking things up and putting them down AKA weightlifting. I also love reading, going to Cher concerts (I'm a BIG fan!) and I never pass up enjoying the little things like the beautiful sunsets the good ol' mitten state has to offer. I love a good challenge and enjoy learning new things-this is where my quick learning skills come in hand. I'm so excited to jumpstart my career as a technical writer and finally put all this knowledge to use for a real company and clients!

Projects by Yours Truly

Literacy Link: Document Plan

For this project, I managed the document design of a campus newsletter with the guidance of my internship mentor. This included creating a document to outline the project, designing the layout and coming up with content to include. 

Activities Program Proposal

For this project, I was tasked with creating a mock-proposal for a real company that would assist them in some way. I proposed a plan that helped them implement an activities program for their residents. This proposal includes research that highlights the problem, as well as a proposed solution and how to incorporate it in their company.

Board Game Instructional Set Analysis

Over the course of this project, I learned how to analyze and create instruction sets that would better serve their audience. I used the board game Monopoly and assessed the instructions with Norman's Principles of Design. 

These projects are just a tiny glimpse into the work that I can do. I'm confident that the skills I have now can truly be an asset in my career. I am very eager to learn more and will always put my best work forward. I have years of learning ahead of me and hope that I'll be given the chance to grow through experience.
